We may collect different categories of personal data, including:

  • Directly from you: when you register, fill out forms, contact support, participate in surveys or promotions.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on one or more of the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: when you have given us your explicit consent for specific purposes (e.g., marketing).

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

We retain your personal data only for the time strictly necessary to achieve the purposes for which it was collected, in compliance with the following criteria:

  • For the duration of the contractual relationship and to fulfill post-contractual obligations.

  • For a maximum period defined based on the principle of necessity and proportionality (e.g., navigation data, security logs). Typically, data not required for long-term legal or contractual obligations is not kept beyond 24-36 months.

6. Data Subject Rights (GDPR)

As a data subject, you have specific rights regarding your personal data under the GDPR (Articles 15-22):

  • Right of Access: obtain confirmation of the existence of your data and access it.
  • Right to Rectification: correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure ('Right to be Forgotten'): request the deletion of your data (under certain circumstances).
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: request the restriction of processing (under certain circumstances).
  • Right to Data Portability: receive your data in a structured format and transfer it to another controller (if technically feasible).
  • Right to Object: object to processing for legitimate reasons or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: withdraw previously given consent at any time (without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal).
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: file a complaint with the competent data protection supervisory authority.

You can exercise your rights by contacting us at the addresses indicated in section 10.

We adopt appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, loss, destruction, or alteration:

Cookie Policy

1. What are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites visited by the user send and record on their computer or mobile device, to then be re-transmitted to the same sites on the next visit. Thanks to cookies, a site remembers the user's actions and preferences (such as, for example, login data, chosen language, font size, other display settings, etc.) so that they do not have to be indicated again when the user returns to visit said site or navigates from one page to another of it.

Cookies, therefore, are used to perform computer authentication, session monitoring, and storage of information regarding the activities of users accessing a site and may also contain a unique identification code that allows tracking the user's navigation within the site itself for statistical or advertising purposes.

2. Types of Cookies Used

This site uses different types of cookies:

  • Essential Technical Cookies: These cookies are strictly necessary to allow navigation and use of the website (e.g., session cookies for authentication). They do not require the user's prior consent. Without these cookies, some features may not be available.
  • Functionality Cookies: Allow the site to remember the choices made by the user (such as language or other customization preferences) to provide a more personalized and improved navigation. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services have been added to our pages. They require consent if not strictly necessary.
  • Analytical (Performance) Cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the website, collecting information in aggregate and anonymous form on the number of users, pages visited, and traffic sources. We use this data to improve the performance of our site. We may use third-party services (e.g., Google Analytics) configured to reduce the identifying power of cookies (IP anonymization). For these cookies, if anonymized, consent is not strictly necessary, but is often requested for transparency.
  • Profiling/Marketing Cookies (if used): These cookies (ours or third-party) are used to create user profiles in order to send advertising messages in line with the preferences expressed by the user while browsing the web. Currently, we may not use direct profiling cookies. Should we implement them, we will request your explicit consent via a specific banner.
